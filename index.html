Why we're meeting

This offsite aligns Sales, Customer Success, Growth, Marketing, and Growth on a unified sales and adoption strategy — specifically the core product bundles.

The Gap

Today, there's no shared model for how these functions work together. Each is building its own approach — but nobody has agreed who owns what, where the handovers are, or what the customer should actually experience at each stage.

One Strategy

Multiple teams, one strategy. This workshop surfaces the realities, clarifies roles, identifies key initiatives, and produces an aligned one-page strategy document.

Session 1: Kickoff + Framing

Session 2: Context + Expectations

Everyone has seen the pre-work baseline. This session pressure-tests it — surface what's wrong, what's missing, and what matters most.

Format: Facilitator walks the responsibility heat map and four key gaps on screen (12 min). Reactions round — each person responds: what's wrong, incomplete, or most important to fix (25 min, ~4 min each). Gap capture on the board with post-its, clustered into themes (15 min).
Output: Validated baseline. Visible board showing confirmed and contested gaps across functions — ready for problem mapping in Session 5.
Key Questions: Where are the biggest disconnects between what each function thinks it owns vs. what others expect? What did the baseline get wrong?

Session 3: Data + Insights

Ground the discussion in real product usage data

Format: Context on why adoption matters for monetisation (5 min). Data presentation (30 min). Structured discussion (25 min).
Data Topics

MAU trends, retention metrics (NURR, CURR), and product usage patterns — presented live with current data.

Output: Shared understanding of the data landscape — what's working, what's dropping off, and where the biggest gaps are.
Key Question: How do we convert entitled users into activated ones, and what does sustained retention look like?
12:00 – 13:00 Lunch
13:00 – 14:30

Session 4: Mapping the Reality

Two parallel journeys — the end-user in the product, and the customer company adopting the platform

Format: Live end-user journey demo (20 min). Customer adoption journey walkthrough with account examples (20–30 min). Discussion connecting both (20–30 min).
End-User Journey

What does an individual user need to do in the product to become successful? Workflows, critical moments, where they get stuck.

Customer Journey

What does it take for a company to progress from awareness through sustained adoption and expansion? Interventions, systems, luck.

Customer Journey Stages
Awareness → Sales → Onboarding → Activation → Adoption → Expansion → Renewal
Output: Two connected journey maps — end-user success path and company-level adoption path — with success points, drop-offs, and support gaps.
Key Question: Where is nobody helping the user? Where is nobody helping the customer? Where are we relying on luck rather than a system?

Session 5: Problem Mapping

Problems first, then theme overlay and prioritisation for Day 2

Format: All problems collected throughout the day go on the wall first. Then we overlay existing themes (gaps) to sort them into buckets. Orphan problems that don't fit reveal missing themes. Dot-vote on themes to prioritise which carry forward to Day 2.
Framing

"What problems will we face in selling and supporting the perfect customer journey?" — not about product gaps, about the sales, CS, and support process. This is input — leadership decides which themes carry forward.

Output: Prioritised list of themes for Day 2 (top 5) with associated problems. Remaining themes parked.

Session 8: Key Bets + Initiatives

Facilitated working session. Work through the top 5 prioritised themes from Day 1 using problem flowers.

Working Method
  1. Take prioritised themes from Day 1 — one at a time
  2. Problem flower: theme's problems at centre, initiative ideas radiating out
  3. Divide into must-haves and nice-to-haves
  4. Small groups of 2 go deeper on must-haves with bullet points
  5. Assign responsible owner + advisor team per initiative
  6. Dot-voting as input to prioritisation

Next
3–6 months

6–12 months
Output: Must-have initiative list with bullet-point detail. Each initiative has a responsible owner and advisor team identified. Nice-to-haves parked for mid-May.

Session 9: Roles & Operating Model

Finalise the ownership matrix — continuation of Session 8

Format: Finalise the ownership matrix started in Session 8. For each theme: confirm accountable team, review bets, confirm responsible owners and advisor teams (90 min).
Strategy Matrix
  • Theme (gap area) — what must be addressed
  • Accountable team — who owns the theme
  • Problems — prioritised issues within the theme
  • Initiative bets — what we're going to do about it
  • Responsible owner — who delivers each initiative
  • Advisor team — who provides guidance and access
Output: Completed strategy matrix — each theme has an accountable team, prioritised problems, initiative bets with responsible owners and advisor teams.
